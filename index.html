about

A short version.

Hello, I'm Beyza. I grew up design obsessed in a house with exactly one artist, my dad. By three I was sprawled on the floor drawing whatever was on Cartoon Network. By six, kindergarten gave me my first heartbreak. By ten, I was the only girl in my brother's friend group, parked in goal because nobody else wanted to be the goalkeeper.

I got into a university in Istanbul on a full scholarship I didn't really want, and cried about it for a week. Graduated top of my class without really trying, mostly because I'd already decided I was leaving. Three months later, at 22, I landed in San Francisco with three suitcases and a jar of Turkish olives.

First job: Popcorn, a fast delivery startup. Officially Category Manager; in practice, the kind of role that meant "do whatever's on fire today." First five employee. Watched it scale to 150 people and a $30M Series A, then watched it close. That one took a while to come out of. I'd loved it like a person.

So I built my own. LANS, a marketplace for coworking spaces, still live at lans.app. Raised $170K, hosted 30+ events with founders and investors across SF, hit 2,000+ App Store downloads in the first six months. Hard years. Cried plenty.

Then Cornell. This time one I'd applied for on purpose. Moved west coast to east. Spent a year that turned a school into my biggest family, graduated May 15, 2026, and in June I'm flying back to the Bay for the next chapter at Arista Networks. Same three suitcases. Probably the same olives.

Outside of all this: oil painting, piano, and long walks looking for the one good coffee in any new city.
